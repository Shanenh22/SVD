Tooth Extractions

Gentle, comfortable extractions — with sedation available — and an honest assessment of what actually needs to come out.

Home › Services › Tooth Extractions Dallas TX
Who this is for
  • Teeth that are severely decayed and cannot be saved with a crown or filling
  • Teeth with infection that has not responded to root canal therapy
  • Broken teeth where the remaining structure is insufficient to restore
  • Teeth causing crowding, shifting, or damage to neighboring teeth
  • Patients preparing for dentures, implants, or orthodontic treatment

Extraction is always the last resort. When it is necessary, we make it simple.

At Spring Valley Dental, extraction is always a last resort. When a tooth is too damaged, infected, or problematic to save, removing it is the right decision — and doing it promptly prevents further damage to surrounding teeth and bone. We perform both simple extractions (fully erupted teeth) and surgical extractions (impacted or fractured teeth), all under local anesthesia with sedation available for anxious patients.

We'll tell you honestly whether a tooth truly needs to come out, or whether a crown, root canal, or other restorative option can save it. We never recommend extraction when preservation is possible.
Simple extraction: fully erupted tooth removed with forceps under local anesthesia
Surgical extraction: impacted or broken tooth removed through a small incision
Tooth replacement planning discussed at the same visit when applicable
Written aftercare instructions provided — including dry socket prevention
Sedation available for anxious patients or complex cases
Most patients back to normal activities within 2–3 days

What to expect

1

Evaluation & X-rays

We take X-rays to assess the tooth's position, root structure, and surrounding bone. We review the images with you and explain exactly what we see before recommending any treatment.

2

Pre-operative instructions

We review any dietary restrictions, medication interactions, and sedation instructions if applicable.

3

Extraction procedure

Under local anesthesia (with sedation if chosen), the tooth is gently removed. Simple extractions use forceps. Surgical extractions involve a small incision when the tooth is impacted or fractured below the gum line.

4

Post-operative care

Detailed aftercare instructions are provided in writing. We follow up to ensure healing is progressing well.

Not always. We only recommend extraction when there is a clear clinical reason — severe decay with no restorable tooth structure, irreversible infection, or a tooth actively damaging surrounding teeth or bone. We show you your X-rays and explain exactly what we see before any decision is made. Preservation is always our first goal.

Extractions are performed under local anesthesia and should be painless during the procedure. You may feel pressure or movement, but not sharp pain. Oral sedation is available if you're anxious. Post-operative soreness is normal and managed with over-the-counter pain relievers for most patients.

Most patients feel sore and swollen for 2–4 days. We provide detailed aftercare instructions including diet recommendations, activity restrictions, and how to keep the extraction site clean. The most important thing is to avoid dry socket — a painful condition caused by the blood clot dislodging. We guide you through exactly how to prevent it.

For most functional or visible teeth, we discuss replacement options at your consultation — dental implants, a fixed bridge, or a removable partial denture. Leaving a gap long-term can cause neighboring teeth to drift and bone to deteriorate. We help you plan the next step before or at the time of extraction so you are never left without a path forward.

Simple extractions of fully erupted teeth typically range from $150 to $350. Surgical extractions for impacted or fractured teeth range from $275 to $500. We provide an exact estimate based on your X-rays before scheduling anything. Most PPO dental plans cover a significant portion of extraction costs.
