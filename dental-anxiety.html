Dental Anxiety — You Are in the Right Place

Certified conscious sedation, judgment-free care, and a team that works at your pace. We have helped hundreds of anxious patients finally get the care they need.

Home › Dental Anxiety
You are not alone. 36% of Americans experience some level of dental anxiety. The most important step is finding a dental team that treats this as a medical reality, not an inconvenience. That is exactly what we do.

Dental anxiety is real. We have real options.

Dental anxiety is not a character flaw. It is a physiological response that causes avoidance of dental care — sometimes for years at a time. At Spring Valley Dental, we understand this and have built our practice around patients who find dental visits genuinely difficult.

Dr. Houshmand is certified in conscious sedation dentistry. She has helped hundreds of anxious patients — including many who had avoided care for a decade or more — finally get the treatment they needed. Patients describe these appointments as comfortable, manageable, and in many cases far easier than they expected.

What we do differently for anxious patients

We listen first — tell us what scares you, what has gone wrong before, and what you need
We explain every step before we do it — no surprises, no sudden movements
We stop immediately if you raise your hand or signal discomfort
We never rush — appointments for anxious patients are scheduled with extra time
Nitrous oxide available for mild anxiety (you can drive afterward)
Oral conscious sedation available for moderate-to-severe anxiety (you need a driver)
Dr. Houshmand monitors you throughout every sedation appointment
You can bring a trusted person with you for support

Common questions

Questions about dental anxiety and sedation

Very. Research consistently finds that roughly 1 in 3 Americans experiences meaningful dental anxiety, and up to 12% have dental phobia severe enough to avoid care entirely. If this describes you, you are not alone — and you are not the first patient to walk through our door with shaking hands.

We offer nitrous oxide (laughing gas) for mild anxiety — you remain alert and can drive afterward. For moderate-to-severe anxiety, we offer oral conscious sedation — a prescription medication taken before your appointment that produces deep, comfortable relaxation. Dr. Houshmand is certified in conscious sedation and monitors you throughout every appointment.
